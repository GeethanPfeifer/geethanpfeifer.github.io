Beat The Royals

This is a small gambling game I made for fun.

There are three types of bets the player can make, "standard", "spades", or "double-edged spades." For each type of bet, the player chooses a rank up to T, or up to K in the case of spades. The ranks are ordered A-K.

Each hand, five cards are dealt.

The player's score is:

  • Standard: the sum of the cards up to (and including) the rank they chose
  • Spades/Double-edged spades: the sum of the spade cards up to (and including) the rank they chose.

The house's score is:

  • Standard/Double-edged spades: the sum of the royals
  • Spades: the sum of the non-spade royals.

Royals have a value of ten.

The bet payout varies based on the specific bet you choose. For fun, I have put in exactly one bet that is +EV (excluding rounding issues for non-multiple-of-hundred bets); the rest are -EV. Every bet is very close to 0EV though, so it won't be noticeable.

There is a "monte-carlo" simulation of the game here. It's not really monte-carlo, it just generates every possible hand.
